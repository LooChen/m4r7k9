# Writing Style Guide

This file accumulates style rules as we discover them. Add to it whenever something lands wrong.

---

## Punctuation

- **No em dashes.** Ever. Use a comma, a period, or rewrite the sentence. If you feel the urge to use one, that's a signal the sentence needs restructuring.
- Hyphens are fine for compound words (self-image, open-minded, status-tracking).
- Double hyphens (--) are acceptable as a last resort in very informal passages, but prefer restructuring.

---

## Voice

- Warm, honest, self-deprecating without being dismissive.
- Not trying to impress. Authority comes from lived experience, not credentials.
- Write like you're talking to a friend who asked a real question.
- Avoid academic hedging. If something is true to the author's experience, say it plainly.
- Avoid inspirational-poster language. Nothing should sound like a LinkedIn post.

---

## Key Concepts (how to talk about them)

- The **filter**: the mechanism by which the mind assigns a charge to incoming experience. Not a metaphor -- a description of a process.
- The **ego**: not a thing, but a process. Not the villain -- just a mechanism doing its job.
- **Shadow**: the part of ourselves we've stopped looking at. Not dark or dramatic. Just unexamined.
- **Status**: the organizing principle behind the filter's judgments. Treat it neutrally, not moralizingly.
- The **curator**: the ego's active, selecting function -- not just filtering but building identity.

---

## What to Avoid

- Em dashes (see above)
- Overqualifying every claim with "perhaps" and "might" and "in some ways"
- Sentences that could appear on a motivational poster
- Condescension disguised as gentleness
- Resolving tension too quickly -- let the uncomfortable thing sit a beat before moving on

---

## Things That Are Working

- Short paragraphs after a buildup (the one-line paragraph that lands the point)
- Concrete examples before abstract claims, not after
- "I'll be honest about something" as a frame for personal admissions
- Naming the reader's likely resistance directly, then not over-reassuring them
- The preface tone: warm, a little self-deprecating, genuinely not trying to impress
