Part III — The Paths · Chapter 8

Making the World Conform

Coming soon
Coming from Chapter 7 Part II ended with the realms — the self-reinforcing worlds the curator builds over time. Each realm has a ceiling. Each one is a palace that is also a prison. Part III opens with the first and most natural response to that recognition: if the filter is causing suffering, change the circumstances that feed it. Get more of the positive valence, less of the negative. Arrange life to produce more hits. This is the first path, and almost everyone is already on it.
Chapter overview

This is the path most people spend their lives on, often without naming it as a path at all. It just looks like ambition, or self-improvement, or wanting a better life. The strategy is: change the circumstances so the filter produces more positive valence. Get the job, the relationship, the body, the reputation that makes the score go up.

The chapter takes this strategy seriously. It works — temporarily. A genuine win produces a genuine hit. There's nothing dishonest about wanting good circumstances. The problem isn't the strategy itself; it's the structural failure mode that's built into it.

The structural failure: the filter adapts. What produced a hit last year becomes the new baseline this year. The treadmill speeds up. The circumstances have to keep improving just to maintain the same score. This isn't a personal failure — it's how the mechanism is designed. The oscillation from Chapter 2 can't be resolved by winning more, because winning more raises the stakes of losing.

This chapter ends by pointing at the exit: the problem isn't the circumstances, it's the filter interpreting them. Which means the second path works differently.
