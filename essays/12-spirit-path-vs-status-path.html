The Spirit Path vs. The Status Path

Coming soon
Coming from Chapter 11 Chapter 11 exposed the trap: the curator's logic can follow you onto the spiritual path itself, turning dissolution into another status game. Once the trap is seen clearly, a fork becomes visible. Two people can be doing similar things — meditating, reading, working on themselves — and be on fundamentally different paths. One is running on curator's logic, collecting spiritual markers. The other is gradually releasing the curator's grip. This chapter makes that fork explicit and offers some diagnostic signs for which one you're on.
Chapter overview

The status path and the spirit path can look identical from the outside. Both involve growth, effort, change. The difference is in the underlying logic. The status path uses spiritual practice the way it uses everything else: to improve the score, to confirm the self-image, to produce hits and avoid withdrawals. The spirit path is organized around a different question entirely: not "how do I do better?" but "who is doing?"

Some diagnostic signs of which path you're on: Does progress feel like accumulation or like release? When you encounter someone more advanced, do you feel envy or appreciation? Does your practice increase your tolerance for uncertainty, or does it increase your certainty? Does spiritual growth make you easier or harder to be around?

The chapter uses two figures as illustrations: the servant leader archetype (Jesus, Obi-Wan, the mentor who has nothing to prove) as an embodiment of the spirit path, and the spiritual authority archetype (the guru who requires deference, the teacher whose status depends on your progress) as the status path wearing spiritual clothes.

No moral judgment is attached to being on the status path. Most people spend most of their lives there. The chapter just wants to make the fork visible.
