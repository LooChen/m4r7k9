Part I — The Machine · Chapter 5

Three Modes of Reducing Suffering

Draft

There are three common approaches to reducing suffering. They operate at different depths, and understanding the difference between them is most of the work.

To see what this means concretely, it helps to start with a single belief and watch what it generates. Take the belief that being attractive is good. Hold that belief, and now receive a compliment. Receive an insult. What arises isn’t random — it’s structured. The belief is the structure.

Three modes of working with this are possible. The first is to try to control which events arrive — seek more compliments, avoid insults. The beliefs stay. The self stays. The oscillation continues. The second is to loosen the beliefs themselves, so that both the highs and lows lose their charge. The third goes deeper still: loosening the sense of self that the beliefs are attached to.
