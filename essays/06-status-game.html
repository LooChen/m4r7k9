The Status Game

20% complete
In progress

Of all the events we witness and categorize, the ones that relate to human status and competition can often be the most impactful in our lives.

These reactions probably have their origins deep in our evolution and were and are important for us as individuals working with a larger pack or society.

This specific set of reactions are probably the most relevant when we’re creating the narrative of our lives, what that is and who we are, so it’s worth examining fairly closely.

As we interact with other people there’s a beautifully intricate dance of micro interactions as we individually and collectively try to order our society. In order to examine this, once again we’re going to need to make gross oversimplifications of what’s actually happening so that it is digestible. In order to do this we can isolate just the interaction between two people and even further oversimplify to just a single dimension of status.

So when two people are engaging, at first there may be a dance that occurs, hundreds or thousand of subtle queues and signals where each tries to determine where the other is at and how the relate or “stack up” to that individual. Through the course of these interactions, eventually across many dimensions, there will start to emerge a relationship. Within those dimensions there we be individual and shared understandings of where each individual “sits” in the hierarchy compared to the other.

Again, I need to emphasize the pretty egregious oversimplification we’re going to have to make here, but bear with me.

So within a dimension, we could say that someone ends up “on top” and the other in the interaction ends up “on the bottom”. A winner, a loser, a teacher, a student, etc, etc.

With our new, vastly oversimplified framing, we can really start to reveal the beauty of this relationship.

When people interact they can come into the interaction with varying degrees of clarity and agreement on who is the “teacher” and who is the “student”. In many cases there is an established hierarchy, and the players conform (mostly) to that established and silently agreed upon norm.

Now, for our filter, what that means in some cases is that the “ego” has accepted a teacher as a teacher and is open to their suggestions, teachings, advice, wisdom rather openly without barriers.

Typically what this means is that someone has acquired something you want, or has achieved a level of mastery that you want to achieve, and therefore you want to understand how they’ve done this.

For example:

  • You’re starting a business and you’re seeking advice from a serial entrepreneur to understand what you need to do to be successful.
  • You’re looking for beauty tips from a friend who always looks great and radiates beauty.
  • You attend a martial arts class to learn from an established black belt.
  • You’re attending a talk by a guru who comes from a long lineage of enlightened gurus.

The mind is constantly reviewing these sources for holes, shortcomings, etc, but on whole, once a source is deemed to be above board, the ego relaxes and allows in the information of the teacher without constantly running the script that checks the validity of the source.

This takes us back to me as a source, my writing chops, and my credentials. I don’t really have any, so I’ve offloaded some of that to the LLM transforming my words, although there’s a lot more there for you to dig into.

Mostly, just be mindful of this constant scanning your mind is doing in this regard. If and when you notice, follow that noticing and validate this experiential reality for yourself. Eventually you won’t have to validate any external source to find additional answers.

So now that you’ve (mostly) validated a source, you have a more open orientation to that source. One understands themselves to be the teacher, and one understands themself to be the student and knowledge transfer happens more freely.
