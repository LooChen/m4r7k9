Part II — The Curator · Chapter 6

The Status Game

In progress
Coming from Chapter 5 Chapter 5 showed the three modes of working with suffering. But before going deeper into those modes, it helps to understand why the filter has the shape it does. Of all the events we witness and categorize, the ones that relate to human status and competition can often be the most impactful in our lives. This chapter examines why.
Chapter overview

We begin with something you already know but have never quite named: the constant low-level monitoring of how you’re landing with the people around you. The micro-signals — who speaks first, who defers, whose joke lands, who gets interrupted. This isn’t paranoia. It’s the filter doing exactly what it was built to do.

The filter is organized around status because the pack animal that became you needed status to survive. Not metaphorically — literally. Standing in the group determined access to food, mates, protection. Pride is the felt sense of status gained. Shame is the felt sense of status lost. The oscillation from Chapter 2 isn’t random; it runs along a status axis.

This reframes the entire emotional landscape. Resentment, envy, validation-seeking, the need to be right, the sting of being corrected in public — all of these are the status-tracking system doing its job. They aren’t character flaws. They’re a very old mechanism running in a world it wasn’t designed for.

The chapter closes with one unanswered question: if status determines what the filter keeps and what it discards, who decides which sources of status get accepted? That question points forward to Chapter 7.
