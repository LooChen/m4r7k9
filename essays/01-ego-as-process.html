Part I — The Machine  ·  Chapter 1

The Ego as Process

Most people think of the ego as some beast inside the brain that emerges at inconvenient moments and takes up space. But rather than thinking of the ego as a thing, let's dig into the actual process of our experience — and see how the concept of "ego" might naturally emerge from that process.

The universe is a moving process, with causal activities continuously unfolding. You, as a human, have senses that take in data from that process, and your mind tries to make sense of what it receives. As the result of evolution, culture, and personal experience, you've built up a set of reactions, thoughts, and beliefs about events that color your experience of the world.

In a sense, this bank of responses becomes a filter — a mechanism your mind uses to break apart the continuous process of reality into discrete experiences, each with their own flavor, or valence. Broadly speaking, we can bucket those experienced reactions as good or bad, positive or negative.

The ego isn't a thing inside you. It's the filter itself — the accumulated weight of every judgment your mind has learned to make.

Three Depths of Conditioning

To see how the filter works, it helps to look at some examples. These are gross oversimplifications of the full picture, but they illustrate the chain of events clearly enough.

Cultural & learned

Cultural and childhood conditioning has solidified the belief that being hard-working is good, and that having this trait makes you a good person. So when your boss praises you in front of your coworkers, you have a pleasant sensation in your abdomen and chest that you might describe as pride.

Personal experience

Several years ago, while riding your bike past a large dog, the dog was startled and reactively bit you. Now when you ride by a dog, there's an immediate recoil upon seeing it, coupled with the acknowledgment of the fear and the need to steer clear. The initial appearance of fear is unpleasant, but the valence turns neutral as you recognize it for what it is.

Pre-cognitive & evolutionary

Walking in the woods, the shape of a snake catches your eye — and before you can even form a mental thought, your body clenches and jumps back. You have a jolt sensation throughout your entire body and a rush of adrenaline. No belief, no deliberation. Just reaction.

The Chain

As you can see, these reactions and beliefs can operate at very different depths of the mind. But the underlying structure is the same in each case:

Events unfold → your mind objectifies and conceptualizes the events → your conditioning judges these events → a reaction arises.

That chain, running continuously below awareness, is what we'll be exploring throughout this series. The filter isn't a flaw. It's a feature — one that evolution spent millions of years building. The question is what happens when you start to see it operating.
