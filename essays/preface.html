A Note Before We Begin

60% complete

I'm writing the following for friends and family to convey insights that I've had through meditation. All of these ideas are out there already in some shape or form, but this configuration, this collective "system" is how they came and continue to sit with me. Nothing is rigorously researched, so take them for whatever you will. Hopefully some of it finds you in a place that it can help.

I'm writing this with the help of an LLM, in this case Claude, so many of the words won't be mine. But the point isn't to impress you with my writing (trust me, you wouldn't be 😊) but rather to extract the concepts and attempt to transfer these concepts as my mind holds them.

In some ways, having an LLM for this is perfect, both in timing and in function. Meditative insights arrive at a level below language. Every attempt to communicate them passes through cultural, personal, and linguistic filters that shape them on the way out. An LLM can grasp the concepts and reorganize them in whatever form suits you. You could even ask it to rewrite any of this in a style you find more credible. And as you'll come to find out, this will matter more than you might think.

The ideas also aren't really "mine" as I see it, and there can be trouble in a writer taking ownership and pride in them, or in a reader heaping praise on the writing. Hopefully that will become clear as to why.

This will also probably be a living document, with branches growing off certain concepts as they arise or develop. The format lends itself to that. It's easy to add, revise, and fork, and the LLM makes that kind of ongoing tending practical in a way it wouldn't have been otherwise.

Even if this "system" is just an output of a causal chain with no inherent design or designer (I'm not making any claims in this regard), what emerged struck me as profoundly beautiful, and seeing it made navigating life a better experience with less suffering. Although I doubt these concepts will feel as profound for you as they did when they came to me, my hope is that they provide another way of seeing, one that maybe helps you on your path to finding your own insights.

I have no formal education, research, PhD, or credentials in any of this. Only experiential knowledge. Hopefully any validation of these concepts comes from your own real-world experience, from testing these ideas out for yourself. Besides, when it comes to deeper wisdom any concepts are just pointers to the experience, which you'll need to undertake yourself. My hope is that this is just another pointer to encourage experiencing.
