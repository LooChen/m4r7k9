Part III — The Paths · Chapter 10

De-objectifying the Self

Coming soon
Coming from Chapter 9 Chapter 9 described dissolving individual beliefs — loosening the charge that makes pride and shame fire so automatically. That is a real path and it goes somewhere. But there is a deeper version of the same move. Not just dissolving the beliefs, but seeing the perceiver of beliefs differently. Chapter 9 works on the contents of the filter. Chapter 10 looks at the filter itself — and the one who seems to be behind it.
Chapter overview

The filter, as described in Chapter 1, takes in events and assigns them meaning. But who is on the receiving end of that meaning? The implicit assumption running through Parts I and II is that there is a fixed self — a "you" — who the filter is serving and protecting. This chapter examines that assumption directly.

What meditation practice tends to reveal at a certain depth is that the self is not a fixed object. It's another process — like the filter itself, like the events the filter interprets. The "I" that feels pride, that suffers shame, that the curator is building and defending — seen closely enough, it doesn't have the solidity it appeared to have.

This is Buddhist territory — the concept of anatta, or not-self — but the chapter approaches it without requiring the framework. The invitation is to test it experientially: look for the fixed self. See what you find. The chapter shares what the author found, without insisting the reader find the same thing.

This is the deepest move in Part III. It doesn't replace Chapter 9 — most people will find Chapter 9 more immediately accessible. But for those drawn further, this is where the path goes.
