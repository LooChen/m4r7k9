Part II — The Curator · Chapter 7

The Realms

Coming soon
Coming from Chapter 6 Chapter 6 showed a single costly moment: the curator refusing wisdom because it arrived from a low-status source. That was one instance. This chapter zooms out and asks: what happens when the same pattern repeats for years? When the curator makes the same kinds of selections — keeping what confirms the self-image, discarding what challenges it — over a lifetime? The answer is that you don't just develop habits. You build a world. The realms are those worlds.
Chapter overview

Buddhist cosmology describes multiple realms of existence — god realms, human realms, hungry ghost realms, hell realms — as places a being can inhabit across lifetimes. This chapter reframes those realms as something closer and more immediate: the self-reinforcing worlds we construct through years of repeated, status-organized curation.

Each realm has its own logic. Its own values. Its own definition of winning and losing. The realm of achievement has a different status hierarchy than the realm of victimhood, but both are organized around status. Both have a ceiling. Both feel, from the inside, like simply reality.

Importantly, no realm is presented as superior to another. The person in the god realm and the person in the hell realm are both running the same underlying mechanism. The realms differ in their texture and their costs, not in their fundamental structure. All of them are the filter at work over a long time horizon.

The chapter ends Part II by showing what the machine builds at scale — and plants the question that opens Part III: given this, what can actually be done?
