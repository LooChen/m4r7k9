Part II — The Curator · Chapter 5

The Curator

Coming soon
Coming from Chapter 4 Part I showed the machine: a filter built to track status, running an addiction loop below the level of conscious choice. Part II opens with a new question. The filter doesn't just react passively to events — it actively builds something. Over time, the accumulated reactions, the kept moments and discarded ones, construct an identity. This chapter names the mechanism that does that building: the curator. It selects what gets kept, what gets polished, what gets shown, and what gets quietly dropped — all according to one criterion: does this serve the self-image?
Chapter overview

The ego isn't just a filter that colors experience. It's also an archivist — actively maintaining a coherent story about who you are. The curator is the part of the mechanism that does this work. It keeps the memories that confirm your self-image and lets the contradicting ones fade. It selects which experiences become part of your story and which remain peripheral.

The curator's selection criterion isn't truth or accuracy. It's status signal. Experiences that confirm your standing get elevated. Experiences that threaten it get minimized, reframed, or dropped entirely. This is why people can hold clearly inconsistent beliefs about themselves for decades without noticing the contradiction — the curator is actively managing the collection.

This chapter also introduces the teacher/student dynamic as a clean example of how the curator decides what to accept. When you encounter someone whose standing you recognize — whose status in a relevant domain you grant — the curator opens the door. The same information from someone whose status you don't recognize gets refused. The credential is checked before the content.

This sets up Chapter 6 directly: if the curator checks credentials before content, what happens to wisdom that arrives without credentials?
